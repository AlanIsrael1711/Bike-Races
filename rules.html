BIKE RACE SAFETY RULES

Regulations & Safety Protocols

Mountain biking is an inherently risky activity. These Safety Rules are not a guarantee against injury. Riders are solely responsible for their equipment and conduct. Your safety is a shared responsibility.

  1. 1. Rider Responsibility

    Each rider is expected to:

    • Inspect equipment before each race, ensuring:
      • Proper brake function (front and rear)
      • Securely fastened wheels
      • Intact drivetrain and suspension components
    • Be self-reliant:
      • Carrying basic repair tools (tube, pump, multi-tool)
      • Knowing basic first aid procedures
    • Respect all race protocols (briefings, start procedures, course markings).

  2. 2. Equipment & Safety Gear

    2.1 Helmets

    • Must meet: CPSC (USA), EN1078 (EU), ASTM F1952 (Downhill).
    • Must fit snugly and be worn at all times while riding.

    2.2 Protective Gear

    • Recommended for all, mandatory for youth: Gloves, Eye protection, Pads.
    • Enduro/Downhill: Full-face helmets are mandatory.

  3. 3. Course Conduct

    3.1 On-Course Behavior

    • Stay within marked boundaries. Yield to emergency personnel.
    • Prohibited: Cutting switchbacks, deliberate obstruction, aggression.

  4. 4. Weather & Environmental Conditions

    • Director may delay, alter or cancel due to lightning, hail or extreme heat.
    • Riders must bring appropriate layers and know signs of hypothermia.

  5. 5. Accidents, Crashes & Injuries

    • First rider on scene must stop and notify a marshal.
    • Head injuries require medical clearance before continuing.

  6. 6. Prohibited Substances & Behavior

    • Zero tolerance for alcohol, PEDs, or recreational drugs.
    • Disqualification for: Abusive language, tampering or littering.

  7. 7. Authority of Race Officials

    • Officials may issue penalties or enforce immediate disqualification.
    • All decisions by the Race Director are final.

  8. 8. Final Acknowledgment

    By registering, riders agree that:

    • They have read, understood, and will comply with all safety regulations.
    • Participation is voluntary, and all inherent risks are accepted.
